SprayFight System

The Graffiti Battle System by TORB

Idea

Graffiti always grabs attention — with its scale, colors, and detail. But creating it often takes a long time, and for viewers the process can feel slow: a piece can be worked on for hours or even all day.

At the same time there’s bombing — fast graffiti where the point isn’t meticulous rendering but the act itself: “I was here.” It can be tags, throw-ups, simple letters, or characters.

People love watching how graffiti is made, and writers love to compete — who does it better, faster, stronger. Battles have existed for years, but most often they run without a system — every city and country has its own way.

System Goal

  • Make the process exciting to watch for the audience;
  • Make it engaging for writers to compete and win;
  • Keep everything dynamic, spectacular, and easy to follow — while preserving the raw street spirit.

Format

16 writers participate. A bracket is formed and each battle is a single round — winners advance until the final. Every round brings a new task and strict time limits that simulate real street bombing conditions.

Rounds

Top 16

  • Time: 30 seconds
  • Task: write a random word (tag)
  • Goal: show style, speed, confidence

Top 8

  • Time: 1 minute
  • Task: random word as a throw-up or simple letter style
  • Color: one

Top 4

  • Time: 1 minute 30 seconds
  • Colors: two
  • Task: random word, more complex form
  • Judging: dynamics, balance, cleanliness

Final

  • Time: 3 minutes
  • Colors: up to four
  • Writers may swap names
  • Creativity, confidence, and skill decide everything

(An optional third-place battle can be added.)

Judging

A panel of 3 or 5 respected figures in different parts of graffiti culture. The key is independence and diverse viewpoints. Judges evaluate:

  • letter style and form;
  • energy and confidence;
  • delivery and originality.

Organization

If there’s no wall nearby, a quick frame or swap panels are used. After each battle, helpers or the writers themselves cover the background so the next work is clearly visible. Perfect buffing isn’t required — it’s enough to prep the surface for the next round.

This keeps the format fast, fresh, and dynamic.

Why it Works

I’ve been running graffiti battles for over five years and I see how this format truly pulls people into the culture. Viewers don’t leave — if anything, the crowd grows with each round.

It’s not just a contest — it’s a living process where art is born in front of you. The format makes graffiti closer and clearer to the public, and for writers it becomes powerful motivation.

Many, after taking part or even just after watching, start to level up, develop their style, or pick up a can for the first time.

This system doesn’t just reveal the strongest — it helps grow a new generation of graffiti writers while preserving the street spirit and respect for the culture.
